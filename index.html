Through the Moss

The Moss Arts Center is one of the more unique buildings on campus. However, pictures you usually see are of the main part of the building. Here, you get to see the architecture of the building from a new viewpoint.

The Blacksburg Frogs

Throughout Blacksburg, there are many frogs sitting outside of different landmarks. This picture captures the frog outside of the Moss Arts Center that watches over Alumni Mall.

Skipper Jr.

A longstanding tradition of Virginia Tech is the cannon, "Skipper," firing at football games. This cannon outside of the Cadet dorms is a smaller version, but the picture shows the view the cannon would see.

Star-Spangled Banner

Addison Caldwell, the first student at Virginia Tech, has a statue looking up at the Cadet halls. This picture takes his perspective as he looks up at the American flag.

H-O-K-I-E-S HOKIES

Looking at principles of design, this picture shows continuity. All of the separate branches of the bush create the Virginia Tech logo in front of Alumni Mall.

Burnt Orange in Nature

Virginia Tech's colors are Chicago Maroon and Burnt Orange. These flowers have the orange effect as they poke out from the flower bed in front of the pylons.

Dorm Life

On the residential side of campus, the residence halls have ornate designs in the Hokie stone. West Eggelston Hall has this seal and gargoyle in front of it, just above the wind tunnel.

Up at Burruss

Pictures of Burruss Hall are all the same. The picture of the front from the Drillfield. This picture gives a personal look at teh building from underneath the legendary tower.

That's not Hokie Stone

There is a rule on campus now that states that all new buildings must use Hokie stone. However, Randolph Hall still is made of brick which creates contrast with the rest of the buildings in the area.

Left, Right, Left

Virginia Tech's Corps of Cadets typically have demonstrations on the Drillfield. This pictures catches them marching off in different groups and showing the tradition of the ROTC.

Torg, Close and Personal

Like the picture of Burruss Hall, the Torg Bridge always has the same typical pictures taken of it. However, this gives a new angle on the legendary bridge.

Even God is a Hokie

In the fall, even the trees turn maroon and orange. The Drillfield starts to show its school spirit in October when the leaves turn Hokie colors.
